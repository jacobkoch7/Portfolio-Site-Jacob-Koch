About The Project

This project involves adapting the "Every Noise All At Once" website into a native app for music discovery. The goal is to provide a seamless integration with popular music streaming platforms while reimagining the brand's UI for mobile devices. The app will focus on optimizing fonts, icons, and other design elements to enhance the user experience on different types of smartphones.

Inspiration

For Every Noise All At Once, I began by researching native design guidelines for both iOS and Material Design to ensure the app would perform seamlessly across both platforms. Since the app is designed to work alongside popular streaming services like Apple Music and Spotify, I drew inspiration from their intuitive layouts and user-focused experiences.

Logos

For the logo, I wanted to capture the idea of mapping music and exploring how genres connect. I combined a gridded sphere to represent structure and exploration with a CD to tie it back to music. Together, they create a design that reflects the app’s purpose: making music discovery feel intuitive and exciting.

Medium Wireframes

Using this inspiration, I began by making early wireframes for both iOS and Material Design, with a strong focus on creating a fluid, cohesive user experience across different platforms. The goal was to ensure the app’s layout would adapt seamlessly to both iOS and Android, while maintaining a consistent, intuitive flow for users.

High Wireframes

Once the wireframes were finalized, I moved on to developing high-fidelity designs. This step involved refining the visual elements, including typography, color schemes, and iconography, to ensure they aligned with the app’s modern, elegant aesthetic.
